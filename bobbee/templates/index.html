How account intelligence works

Sorting accounts into cadences follows five transparent scoring and scheduling stages, so you can see why an account landed where it did.

  1. Contacts. Every account is checked for a real IT decision-maker. Accounts without one are set aside into No contacts rather than being worked blind.
  2. Scoring. Account Tiering scores what's left on IBM spend and trend, install base, company size, and recent buying signals.
  3. Quarter segmentation. Accounts are split across the four quarters by their buying signals. Only the current quarter's accounts continue; the rest are saved to Future quarters.
  4. Cadence and rank. Each account is matched to a play, ranked within its cadence, and tagged. Accounts past the per-cadence cap drop to Leftovers and rejoin the pool for a future quarter.
  5. Distribution. Cadence starts are spread across the quarter's weekdays, and each account's touches are laid on its cadence's step days — producing the day-by-day schedule.

Where AI is used

The colour convention runs through the whole app: blue is deterministic application data, purple with a sparkle is generated by watsonx.ai. Only two things are model-generated:

  • Play and sales angle on the account detail popup.
  • Pre-call briefs on the Call tab.

Everything else — scoring, ranking, cadence assignment, the schedule, email drafts — is deterministic Python. If watsonx is unreachable or has no key, both features fall back to deterministic output built from the same account data, so the app keeps working.
